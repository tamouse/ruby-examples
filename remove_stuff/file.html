3. Summary of Issues in Dispute
The issue is whether the Applicant prematurely commenced this arbitration proceeding.
4. Findings, Conclusions, and Basis Therefor
The EIP was involved in a motor vehicle accident on August 26, 2010. Therein, he
purportedly sustained personal injuries. The Applicant delivered items of durable medical
equipment to the EIP on October 11, 2010. The billed cost for the items delivered on this
date was $1084.95. The Applicant also indicated that they delivered durable medical
equipment to the EIP on August 30, 2010 (see the Arbitration Request Form). A bill for the
August 30, 2010 supplies was not within the Electronic Case Folder, and proof that such a
2
bill was presented to the Respondent is also absent from the record. The claim, in view of
the lack of proof, is only in regard to the October 11, 2010 supplies.
These items of supply were, purportedly, necessary to help treat injuries sustained in the
subject motor vehicle accident. The billing claim form regarding the October 11, 2010
supplies was presented to the Respondent, and its receipt was acknowledged by the
Respondent. The Respondent did not challenge the Applicant’s prima facie entitlement to
recover No-Fault benefits with respect to the items of supply that were delivered on October
11, 2010.
The Respondent did, however, argue that they did not have to pay or deny the Applicant’s
claim. They requested additional verification to establish proof of claim from the Applicant.
They then contended that the materials were never furnished and that, therefore, they were
under no obligation to pay or deny the claim.
The information sought included an assignment of benefits form, various corporate
documents and agreements, prescriptions, delivery receipt, wholesaler information, and
instructions for DME use. The requests were made to the Applicant in accordance with 11
NYCRR 65-3.5, and when the Applicant failed to respond to the first request, the Respondent
‘followed-up’ and sent a second request as per the requirements of 11 NYCRR 65-3.6. The
verification requests were proper in all respects.
11 NYCRR 65-3.8(3) directs that a denial shall not be served prior to the receipt of all
relevant information requested. However, on February 7, 2013, the Applicant mailed a
response to the Respondent regarding their verification requests. The response addressed all
of the items listed within the verification requests. The Respondent did not take any action
after they received the Applicant’s responses, i.e., they did not pay or deny the claim, nor did
they request any other verification.
The Respondent, if they had an issue regarding whether the information received fully
complied with their request, should have informed the Applicant of what was not furnished.
Any confusion as to what is sought in a verification request should be addressed by further
communication, not inaction. Westchester County Medical Center v. N.Y. Central Mutual
Life Insurance Co., 262 A.D.2d 553, 555, 692 N.Y.S.2d 665, 667 (2d Dept. 1999).
Based upon the foregoing, the Respondent should have paid or denied the Applicant’s claim.
Since the Applicant’s prima facie entitlement to recover the cost of the delivered items of
supply was established, the claim is awarded.
All evidence submitted herein by the Applicant and the Respondent is contained within the
Electronic Case Folder maintained by the American Arbitration Association, as of the date of
the hearing.
